## Hospital Management System

The Hospital Management System is designed to manage patients, doctors, appointments, prescriptions, and billing. The system keeps track of patient appointments, doctor consultation, medicines prescribed by doctors, and bills generated for patients.

### Main Entities

The system will have the following main entities:

1. **Patient**
   A patient represents a person who visits the hospital for treatment or consultation.

2. **Doctor**
   A doctor represents a medical professional who consults patients. Each doctor has a specialization and consultation fee.

3. **Appointment**
   An appointment represents a meeting between a patient and a doctor on a particular date and time.

4. **Prescription**
   A prescription represents the diagnosis, medicines, and advice given by a doctor after consultation.

5. **Bill**
   A bill represents the charges generated for a patient after an appointment is completed.

---

### Relationships

1. **Patient to Appointment**
   One patient can have multiple appointments.

2. **Doctor to Appointment**
   One doctor can have multiple appointments.

3. **Appointment to Prescription**
   One completed appointment can have one prescription.

4. **Appointment to Bill**
   One completed appointment can have one bill.

---

### Appointment Rules

The system should support appointment booking between patients and doctors.

1. A patient can book an appointment with a doctor.

2. A doctor should not have two appointments at the same date and time.

3. A patient should not have two active appointments with the same doctor on the same date.

4. Only scheduled appointments can be cancelled.

5. Only scheduled appointments can be marked as completed.

Example:

If Doctor A already has an appointment at 10:30 AM on 10 July 2026, another patient cannot book Doctor A at the same date and time.

---

### Prescription Rules

Prescription should be created only after appointment completion.

1. Prescription cannot be created for a scheduled or cancelled appointment.

2. One appointment can have only one prescription.

3. Prescription should contain diagnosis and medicine details.

Example:

If Patient A completed an appointment with Doctor B, then Doctor B can create a prescription with diagnosis, medicine name, dosage, and advice.

---

### Billing Rules

Bill should be generated after appointment completion.

1. Bill cannot be generated before appointment completion.

2. Consultation fee should come from the doctor.

3. Total bill should include consultation fee, medicine charges, lab charges, and other charges.

Formula:

Total Bill = Consultation Fee + Medicine Charges + Lab Charges + Other Charges

4. Paid amount cannot be greater than total bill amount.

5. Payment status should be shown as:

* Pending
* Partially Paid
* Paid

Example:

Total Bill = ₹1000
Paid Amount = ₹500
Payment Status = Partially Paid

---

### Business Rules

1. If a doctor is inactive, appointment should not be booked with that doctor.

2. If a doctor already has an appointment at a selected time, booking should not be allowed.

3. If appointment is cancelled, prescription and bill should not be created.

4. If patient has more than 2 pending bills, system should show a warning while booking a new appointment.

5. Patient history should not be permanently deleted.

---

### Soft Delete

Important records should not be permanently deleted from the database.

Soft delete should be used for:

* Patient
* Doctor
* Appointment
* Prescription
* Bill

When a record is deleted, it should be marked as:

`IsDeleted = true`

This helps preserve medical and billing history.

---

### Reports

The system should provide the following reports:

#### 1. Doctor-wise Appointment Report

Show how many appointments each doctor handled in a selected date range.

Example:

* Doctor A: 50 appointments
* Doctor B: 35 appointments

---

#### 2. Patient Appointment History Report

Show all appointments of a selected patient.

Example:

Patient A:

* 1 July 2026: Appointment with Doctor X
* 10 July 2026: Appointment with Doctor Y

---

#### 3. Doctor Revenue Report

Show total consultation revenue generated by each doctor.

Formula:

Doctor Revenue = Completed Appointments × Consultation Fee

Example:

Doctor A completed 20 appointments.
Consultation Fee = ₹500
Revenue = ₹10,000

---

#### 4. Pending Bill Report

Show patients whose bills are not fully paid.

Example:

Patient A:

* Total Bill: ₹2000
* Paid: ₹1000
* Pending: ₹1000

---

#### 5. Most Prescribed Medicine Report

Show medicines that are prescribed most frequently.

Example:

* Paracetamol: 100 times
* Antibiotic: 60 times

---

#### 6. Appointment Status Report

Show appointment count status-wise.

Example:

* Scheduled: 20
* Completed: 80
* Cancelled: 10

---

#### 7. Patients With More Than 2 Pending Bills

Show patients who have more than 2 unpaid or partially paid bills.

Example:

Patient A has 3 pending bills, so show this patient in the report.

---

### LINQ Query Scenarios

The system should support LINQ queries for:

1. Show doctor-wise appointment count for last month.

2. Show patient-wise pending bill amount.

3. Show top 5 doctors by revenue.

4. Show appointment-status-wise count.

5. Show patients who have more than 2 pending bills.

6. Show most prescribed medicines.

7. Show patients who had appointment but prescription was not created.

8. Show completed appointments where bill was not generated.

---

### Overall System Goal

The main goal of this system is to manage patient appointments, doctor consultations, prescriptions, billing, and hospital reports. The system should prevent duplicate appointment booking, maintain patient history, track pending bills, and generate useful reports for hospital management.
